05 Summer 2021

Gary the Octopus

Graphic, Publication (booklet), Character

Team Project - Zen Maker Lab

Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator


Project Description

In order to promote the STEAM summer camp for the kids of Zen Maker Lab, my team had to create an activity booklet that includes the safety comics and sticker collection. My role in this project was to design the appearance of Gary and the achievement badges. After a few iterations and discussions, we designed an octopus character called Gary as the main character of the activity booklet and the mascot of Zen Maker Lab. We also designed the cartoon version of the project kits that will be taught during the summer camp as Gary's friends. We then designed the achievement badges and the comics based on Gary's appearance by using PhotoShop and Illustrator.

Final iteration of the activity booklet cover. [jpg]

Design Process

I first researched what features a cartoon should have for kids, and I found out that kids usually tend to like cartoons with big eyes. I drew different kinds of octopus sketches to try how does the size of the eyes affects the appearance of a character.

Sketches of Gary. [PDF]

After having peer review on our sketches, we all agreed that big eyes are a must feature that Gary should have. Besides, we discussed that Gary should have a big round head, and the length of the tentacles should be longer so that Gary can "play" with his friends - the project kits.

The final version of Gary. [PDF]

Based on the final version of Gary, we then started to sketch the 6 categories of achievement badges, which are building, film and animation, coding, creativity, engineering and values, and each badge should be related to Gary.

The first iteration of the badges. [PDF]

To identify the categories, we used the colours that are listed in the Zen Maker Lab's branding guideline, which is purple, orange, yellow, green, and red, and we used blue for the "values" category.

Second Iteration of badges. [PDF]

Before we printed it out on the vinyl printer, we tried to make changes based on the peer review and added colours to the badges

Part of the finalized achievement badges. [PNG]

Challenges

The most challenging part of this project was the tight deadlines. This project started at the end of May. As the summer camp started at the end of June and I have other positions in Zen Maker Lab, we have to rush to finish finalizing Gary as well as the badges so that we could distribute them to the camps on time.

Reflection

From this project, not only I improved my technical skills in Illustrator and Photoshop, but I also gained more experience in communicating and coordinating with my teammates. They often provided feedback and critique on the design, it helped me to understand what was doing great and what was not working for the target audience - the kids.
