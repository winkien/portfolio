NOMI

Graphics + Publication (magazine)

Individual Project

Adobe Photoshop, InDesign


Project Description

In the Graphic Design in Transition: Print and Digital Periodicals course, I had to create a magazine for both print and online. This project includes the cover, table of contents (TOC), editorial story, business card, online advertisement (box and tower) and online article. I created NOMI, a home decor magazine that focuses on interior design and decorating across Canada. NOMI is a Japanese word, not only stands for only one, unique and important, but also means beautiful, pleasant and delightful. I think this name matches the theme of my magazine as it will combine aesthetics, design trends and decorating advice for readers to explore and get inspiration for their unique home, and create their comfort zone and dream house.

Design Process

I first thought about what kind of magazine I would like to design. I chose home decor magazine because I was interested in interior design and wanted to explore how to create an effective home decor magazine. As my magazine aims to inspire and guide the readers to make their home a more comfortable, unique and delightful place, I searched online to see if any words have this kind of meaning, and I found NOMI. After that, I used a blinkplan to organize my magazine, such as the section and article’s name and created a mood board and template of the cover, TOC, and story. Then, I picked an article about Modern Bohemian Style as my cover editorial story and used the images in copyright-free websites to create the cover and story spread based on the template. Lastly, I created the subscription box and tower advertisement, business card, a sidebar, and two online editorial adaptations (one is the article, and one is the quiz to test which kind of decorating style suits you).

Challenges

Creating the TOC is the most challenging and difficult task for me because it is typographically and hierarchically complicated. When I first designed the TOC, I wanted to place all of them on one page as my magazine doesn’t have too many articles so it should be ok to place them on one page. However, I realized that it is very hard to do so as there aren't many white spaces between the images and texts. Thus, I used 2 pages to create TOC to make it more organized in a hierarchical and easy-to-follow way.

Reflection

The project allows me to understand how a new publication developed to the public, such as how to design an effective magazine - its brand, editorial, and marketing collateral - to gain reach and generate cross-platform traffic. I also learn the principles of both audience development and art direction, and how to effectively design and package editorial content for both print and online.
