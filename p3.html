03 Summer 2020

Soap Man

Book + Graphics

Individual Project


Project Description

In the Graphic Design Fundamentals course, I had to create a booklet and a poster that is related to COVID-19 and contains a call-to-action message by using InDesign and Illustrator. I created Soap Man, and its central idea is to encourage people to wash their hands with soap often. The target audience would be those who do not know what to do during COVID-19.

Final iteration of Soap Man. [PDF]

Design Process

I first researched some COVID-19 information from the government websites to get some inspiration and brainstormed ideas. I chose “washing hands with soap” as my central idea because I always touch my face without realizing it and I think it is important for people to know how soap can help us prevent infection. I drew 30 sketches to explore the feeling of the poster. After receiving feedback from the professor and peers, I picked the best 3 sketches and roughly refined them to create a larger detailed layout. Lastly, I chose the best solution among the 3 roughs and created a booklet with cover, front matter and back matter.

Challenges

The main challenge throughout this project is how to create a unique and attractive poster. At the beginning of this project, I was stuck when I was drawing the 30 sketches because I realized that my sketches are not very attractive, which will be hard to educate kids because they may not understand the meaning. In order to make my poster more engaging, I created a Soap Man instead of just a gun pointing at the virus so that the central idea of the poster will be able to convey the kids better.

The cover of the booklet. [PDF]

Reflection

Throughout this project, I learnt that it is important to put myself into the shoe to think about how the design should look so that it can communicate the central idea to the target audiences effectively. In this case, I chose to use graphics instead of an informative poster because graphics are more engaging to the kids so that the message can be effectively understood by the kids.
